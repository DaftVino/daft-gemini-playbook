# 049 — Strategic Board / Stakeholder Update

- **Category:** Executive Leadership
- **Surface:** Gemini in Docs · Gemini in Gmail
- **Risk:** Yellow/Red
- **Owner:** Executive
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-07-21
- **Required sources:** @KPI Pack, @Financial Update, @Project Tracker, @Risk Register, @Prior Update
- **Core blocks:** Evidence, Finance, Legal/People/Risk

## When to use

A periodic update is due to a board, ownership, or a lender.

## Prompt

```text
Using @KPI Pack, @Financial Update, @Project Tracker, @Risk Register, and @Prior Update, prepare a
[board / ownership / lender] update for [period].

Cover: performance against plan, material changes since the last update, risks and how they have
moved, decisions requested, forecast and cash impact, and next milestones.

Return three versions:
(1) An email version under 400 words
(2) A slide-ready version of eight bullets
(3) Likely questions with evidence-based answers, including the three questions we would least like
    to be asked

Also return:
(4) A list of every commitment made in @Prior Update, with its current status. An update that
    quietly drops last period's commitments is the fastest way to lose a board's confidence.
(5) Any figure in the update that is not yet final, marked as preliminary

Preserve uncertainty — do not round a range into a point estimate. Do not make a representation
about future performance, covenant compliance, or financial position beyond what the sources
establish.
```

## Validation before use

- **Escalates to Red for any lender or external stakeholder communication. Authorized reviewer:
  Executive, with the Controller confirming every figure, and counsel where representations are
  made.**
- Every number is tied to the financial update before sending. A corrected board figure is
  expensive.
- Check section 4 personally. Dropped commitments are the most common credibility failure and the
  model will surface them if asked.

## Example follow-up

`What are the three hardest questions this update invites, and what is the honest answer to each?`
