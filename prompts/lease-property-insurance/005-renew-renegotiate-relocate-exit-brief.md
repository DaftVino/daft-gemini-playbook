# 005 — Renew, Renegotiate, Relocate, or Exit Brief

- **Category:** Lease, Property & Insurance
- **Surface:** Gemini in Docs
- **Risk:** Yellow
- **Owner:** Property lead
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-07-21
- **Required sources:** @Lease Abstract, @P&L, @Sales Trend, @Repair History, @Landlord Correspondence, @Alternative Sites
- **Core blocks:** Evidence, Finance

## When to use

A notice deadline is approaching and the site's future needs a decision with a written rationale.

## Prompt

```text
Prepare a decision brief for [site] using @Lease Abstract, @P&L, @Sales Trend, @Repair History,
@Landlord Correspondence, and @Alternative Sites. Compare four options: renew, renegotiate,
relocate, and exit.

Return:
(1) A five-bullet recommendation
(2) An occupancy-cost comparison across the four options, showing total occupancy cost and
    occupancy cost as a percentage of revenue, with your calculation shown
(3) Operational factors that are not in the numbers
(4) Our leverage and the landlord's leverage, each with the evidence for it
(5) Risks and what would change the recommendation
(6) Assumptions, labelled separately from facts
(7) The opening negotiating position and the walk-away point
(8) The decision deadline, derived from the notice requirement

Label every figure as source-supported or estimated. Do not present an estimate as a fact.
```

## Validation before use

- Re-derive the decision deadline from the lease itself, not from the abstract.
- Check that the P&L scope matches the site and period being analyzed — multi-entity allocations
  routinely make one site look better or worse than it is.
- Relocation and exit costs are almost always understated on the first pass. Confirm restoration,
  holdover, and remaining-term exposure are included.
- Controller reviews the occupancy-cost math before this goes to a decision forum.

## Example follow-up

`What would have to be true about the next 24 months of sales for "renew" to beat "relocate"?`
