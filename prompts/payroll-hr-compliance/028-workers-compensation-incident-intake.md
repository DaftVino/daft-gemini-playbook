# 028 — Workers' Compensation Incident Intake

- **Category:** Payroll, HR & Compliance
- **Surface:** Gemini in Docs
- **Risk:** Red
- **Owner:** HR lead
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-07-21
- **Required sources:** @Incident Report, @Witness Notes, @Photos, @Schedule, @Policy
- **Core blocks:** Evidence, Legal/People/Risk

## When to use

An injury has been reported and reporting deadlines are already running.

## Prompt

```text
Organize initial incident information from @Incident Report, @Witness Notes, @Photos, @Schedule,
and @Policy.

Return:
(1) A fact timeline with the source for each entry
(2) Medical and emergency actions already taken, as reported
(3) An evidence-preservation list: scene condition, equipment, footage, schedules, training
    records, and who must preserve each
(4) Reporting deadlines to confirm — carrier notification, regulatory reporting, internal
    escalation — stated as "confirm this deadline applies," not as established
(5) Missing facts and who can supply them
(6) A factual internal summary containing only what the sources establish
(7) The points requiring carrier, HR, and legal review

Do not decide compensability. Do not assign fault or characterize conduct. Do not include medical
detail beyond the fact and timing of treatment. Do not speculate about cause. Where the reports do
not establish a fact, state "Insufficient evidence to conclude" and list what would establish it.
```

## Validation before use

- **Authorized reviewer: HR lead, with the carrier. Reporting deadlines are jurisdiction-specific —
  confirm them with HR or the carrier, never from this output.**
- Preservation actions are immediate. Footage in particular is often overwritten within days.
- Verify no medical detail beyond treatment fact and timing has reached the output.
- Nothing in this document should read as a conclusion about fault. It is discoverable.

## Example follow-up

`List only the actions that must happen in the next 24 hours, with the owner for each.`
