# 085 — Performance Review Preparation Pack

- **Category:** Payroll, HR & Compliance
- **Surface:** Gemini in Docs
- **Risk:** Yellow/Red
- **Owner:** HR lead
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-07-21
- **Required sources:** @Job Description, @Performance Standards, @Goal Record, @Documented Feedback, @Objective Results
- **Core blocks:** Evidence, Legal/People/Risk

## When to use

A manager is preparing a scheduled review and needs it grounded in the record rather than the last
three weeks.

## Prompt

```text
Using @Job Description, @Performance Standards, @Goal Record, @Documented Feedback, and @Objective
Results, prepare a review pack for [role] covering [period].

Return:
(1) Performance against each documented goal or standard: Goal | Standard | Result | Evidence and
    its date | Met / Partially met / Not met / No evidence recorded
(2) Contributions supported by the record, with dates
(3) Development areas supported by the record, with dates and any feedback previously given
(4) **Coverage check** — how much of the evidence comes from the last 60 days versus the full
    period. Recency bias is the defining defect of performance reviews and it is measurable.
(5) Goals or standards with no evidence either way, listed explicitly. These are a management gap,
    not a performance finding.
(6) Draft discussion points, each tied to specific evidence
(7) Proposed goals for the next period, measurable and tied to the job description

Use only documented evidence. Do not infer performance from a result the person did not control.
Do not use personality descriptions, comparisons to colleagues, or characterizations of attitude.
Do not recommend a rating, compensation change, promotion, or any employment action. Do not
reference leave taken, medical matters, or any protected characteristic.
```

## Validation before use

- **Escalates to Red where the review may lead to a rating affecting pay, a performance plan, or
  termination. Authorized reviewer: HR lead before the conversation.**
- Check the coverage figure in section 4. If most evidence is recent, gather more before proceeding.
- Section 5 is a finding about management, not about the employee. Read it that way.
- The manager writes the review. This assembles the evidence.

## Example follow-up

`Which statements here rest on evidence, and which are inference? Remove every inference.`
