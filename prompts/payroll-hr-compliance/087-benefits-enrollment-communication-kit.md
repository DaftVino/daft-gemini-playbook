# 087 — Benefits Enrollment Communication Kit

- **Category:** Payroll, HR & Compliance
- **Surface:** Gemini in Docs · Gemini in Gmail
- **Risk:** Red
- **Owner:** HR lead
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-07-21
- **Required sources:** @Plan Documents, @Carrier Summaries, @Enrollment Timeline, @Prior Year Communications, @Eligibility Rules
- **Core blocks:** Evidence, Legal/People/Risk

## When to use

Enrollment is opening and the communication needs to be understandable without becoming inaccurate.

## Prompt

```text
Using @Plan Documents, @Carrier Summaries, @Enrollment Timeline, @Prior Year Communications, and
@Eligibility Rules, prepare an enrollment communication kit for [period].

Return:
(1) An announcement email under 300 words: what is changing, what to do, by when, where to get help
(2) A one-page summary of changes from the prior year, with the source for each
(3) A frequently-asked-questions document built from the questions actually asked last year
(4) A deadline and action calendar, including what happens if someone does nothing
(5) A manager briefing — what managers should and should not say. Managers must not advise on plan
    selection, interpret coverage, or discuss anyone's circumstances.
(6) A list of every statement in the drafts that requires HR or carrier verification before it is
    sent

Rules:
- Use only the plan documents and carrier summaries. Do not describe a benefit, an exclusion, an
  eligibility rule, a cost, or a deadline that is not in them.
- Do not simplify in a way that changes meaning. Where accuracy requires complexity, keep the
  complexity and add "contact HR" rather than producing a clean wrong answer.
- State that plan documents govern.
- Do not give tax or plan-selection advice, or recommend an option.
- Where the plan documents do not answer a question you need to address, state "Insufficient
  evidence to conclude," leave a placeholder, and list it in section 6 for carrier confirmation.
```

## Validation before use

- **Authorized reviewer: HR lead, with carrier or broker confirmation of every plan detail, before
  anything is distributed.** A benefits communication is relied on and creates exposure when wrong.
- Verify every deadline and every cost figure against the source directly.
- Check that no example or scenario implies a recommendation.

## Example follow-up

`List every factual claim in the announcement with the document and page that supports it.`
