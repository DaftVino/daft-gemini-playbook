# 022 — Budget Owner Challenge Pack

- **Category:** Finance & Accounting

- **Risk:** Yellow

- **Required sources:** @Budget Submission, @Prior Year Actuals, @Current Run Rate, @Assumptions, @Strategic Plan
- **Core blocks:** Evidence, Finance

## When to use

A budget submission has arrived and you need to prepare for the review conversation.

## Prompt

```text
Review @Budget Submission, @Prior Year Actuals, @Current Run Rate, @Assumptions, and @Strategic
Plan for [owner / entity].

Return a challenge pack:
(1) The key assumptions the submission depends on, and how much of the result each drives
(2) Trend inconsistencies — where the submission departs from prior actuals or the current run rate
    without a stated reason. Show prior, run rate, and budget side by side.
(3) Missing cost drivers — costs present in actuals but absent or reduced in the budget, with the
    amount
(4) Risks and opportunities not reflected in the numbers
(5) Questions ranked by materiality, phrased to open a conversation rather than to corner the owner
(6) A revised-scenario table: submitted, run-rate-based, and a middle case, with the difference

Keep the tone analytical and collaborative. Do not characterize a submission as optimistic or
padded — show the gap between the submission and the evidence, and let the owner explain it.
```

## Validation before use

- Confirm the run rate is annualized on a comparable basis before presenting any gap.
- Check that known committed changes — a signed contract, a rate increase, a new site — are not
  being flagged as unexplained growth.
- This is a conversation preparation tool, not a verdict. Do not circulate it to the budget owner.

## Example follow-up

`Give me the five questions that matter most, in the order I should ask them.`
