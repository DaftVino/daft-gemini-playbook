# 106 — Shift Huddle Brief

- **Category:** Frontline Enablement
- **Surface:** Gemini in Docs · Gemini in Sheets
- **Risk:** Green
- **Owner:** Field leadership
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-07-21
- **Required sources:** @Site Dashboard, @Action Plan, @Site Notes, @Upcoming Schedule
- **Core blocks:** Evidence, Data quality

## When to use

Before a shift, to turn whatever leadership is tracking into something that can be said out loud in
three minutes.

## Prompt

```text
Using @Site Dashboard, @Action Plan, @Site Notes, and @Upcoming Schedule, write a huddle brief for
[site] for [shift / date].

Return exactly this, and nothing else:

**One thing that went well** — specific, recent, and attributable to the team
**One thing to focus on today** — one, with what good looks like by end of shift
**What is different today** — schedule, deliveries, equipment, visitors, weather, events
**Safety or quality reminder** — one, relevant to today rather than generic
**What to escalate, and to whom**

Rules:
- Three minutes spoken. Roughly 250 words.
- Plain spoken language. Short sentences. No percentages unless the team already uses them.
- One focus item. Two is zero.
- Do not name an individual in connection with anything negative.
- Do not include financial detail beyond what the team can influence during the shift.
- If the dashboard has a data issue, leave it out of the huddle entirely — do not ask a shift team
  to act on a number nobody trusts.
```

## Validation before use

- Read it aloud. If it takes more than three minutes or does not sound like speech, it is wrong.
- Confirm the focus item is something the shift can actually change today.
- Check nothing negative is attributable to an identifiable person.

## Example follow-up

`Rewrite the focus item so anyone on shift can tell by lunchtime whether we are on track.`
